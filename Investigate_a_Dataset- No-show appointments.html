Introduction¶

Dataset Description¶

In this project I will be analyzing medical appointments in Brazil which focused on the question of whether or not patients show up for their appointment as scheduled. I will be using No-show appointment's dataframe gotten from Kaggle for the analysis. The dataframe has significant table columns which are: (1) Patient_id that shows a Unique identifier for the patient. (2) Appointment_id which shows Unique identifier for the appointment. (3) Gender shows Gender of the patient in the dataset. (4) Scheduled day: shows the date appointment was scheduled (5) Appointment day: shows appointment date and time. (6) Age: shows patient age. (7) Neighborhood: Neighborhood hospital is located. (8) Scholarship: the column indicate whether or not the patient enrolled in Brasilian welfare program. The dataframe also contains columns that narrate type of patients illness. (9) Hypertension: One (1) indicate that the patient is Hypertensive while zero (0) indicate not. (10) Diabetes: One (1) indicate that the patient is Diabetics while zero (0) indicate not. (11) Alcoholism: One (1) indicate that the patient have Alcoholism while zero (0) indicate not. (12) Handicap: (1) indicate that the patient is Handicap while zero (0) indicate not. Other columns are: (13) SMS received: One (1) indicate that the patient have received notification while zero (0) indicate not. (14) No_show: NO if patient show for the appointment while YES indicate not.

Question(s) for Analysis¶

  1. what is the effect of elapsed time(day interval between Scheduled day and Appointment day) to not show up for scheduled appointment?
  2. What factors are important for us to know in order to predict if a patient will show up for their scheduled appointment?

Conclusions¶

With the analysis above i was able to discover: (1) With the use of histogram i was able to know that elapsed time have high impact on patients not showing for their appointment. The lower the elapsed time given to the patient the high chances of not showing up for their appointment. (2) By checking the effect of age, gender, scholarship and SMS received against No_show shows that gender and scholarship have little or no effect on showing up for their appointment. The analysis has also revealed that appointment reminder message and age of the patient have effect on patient not showing up. The lower the reminder message the higher the rate of No show and the young patients have high rate of no show, the lower the age the higher the chances of no show.

With the summary above, factors to considered while predicting whether or not patient will show up for their appointments are elapsed time, age and SMS received.

For futher and more detailed prediction, information containing the history of the patients on showing up for the appointment is neccessary and also, patients house location to determine whether patient distance to the hospital is one of the factors or not.
